Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

Overview

CoverMap is an open-source Android app that lets you highlight places on a world map. This policy explains what data the app accesses, how it is used, and how you can request its deletion.

Data the App Accesses

CoverMap uses two Google APIs. No other data is collected.

  • Google Drive API (read/write access to files created by the app): your map highlights are saved as a JSON file (cover-map-highlights.json) in your own Google Drive. This file contains highlight coordinates, colors, and associated photo references. The app only accesses this single file.
  • Google Photos Picker API (read-only): when you choose to attach a photo to a map location, the picker lets you select photos from your library. The app receives only the metadata (media ID, thumbnail URL) of the photos you explicitly select. No photos are uploaded to any server.

What the App Does Not Do

  • No Google Analytics, Firebase Analytics, or any tracking SDK is included.
  • No personal information is collected or transmitted to third parties.
  • No advertising identifiers are used.

Local Storage

All data is also stored locally on your device using browser localStorage. Google OAuth tokens are stored in Android's encrypted shared preferences (AES-256-GCM) and never leave your device except to authenticate with Google APIs.

Deleting Your Data

  • Google Drive: open your Google Drive, locate the cover-map-highlights.json file, and delete it. You can also revoke the app's access in your Google Account permissions.
  • Local data: uninstall the app or clear its storage from your device settings. This removes all locally stored highlights and tokens.

Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top indicates when changes were last made.

Contact

For questions about this privacy policy or to request deletion of your data, open an issue on the GitHub repository.
